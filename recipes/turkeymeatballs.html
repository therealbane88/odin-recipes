Cheesy Turkey Meatballs

Description

Cheesy Turkey Meatballs are a great tasting alternative to traditional meatballs. They are packed with protein the flavor is brought to a new level with the red sauce to give you an overall savory meal for you and your guests or family. And you don't have to serve them just by themselves, pair them with your favorite pasta or side such as breadsticks!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (or 1 tablespoon of pre-minced garlic)
  • 2 Tbsp chopped rosemary
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes (28oz)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1 lb. ground turkey
  • 1 cup bread crumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 Tbsp chopped parsley, plus some extra for garnish
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 deg. Make the sauce while oven preheats: In a large pot over low heat add 1/4 cup of oil. Add half of the garlic and all of the rosemary and stir, being careful not to burn the garlic or rosemary, until fragrant, should take around 1 minute. Add the one can of crushed tomatoes and season with salt and pepper. Let sauce simmer while you make the meatballs.

  2. In a large bowl, mix together the ground turkey, egg, bread crumbs, parsley, and the rest of the garlic and season with salt and pepper. Roll the mixtures into meatballs and set aside.

  3. In a large skillet, over medium-high heat, heat remaining 2 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil. Add meatballs and brown, occasionally moving them around to prevent sticking, about 5 minutes.

  4. With a ladle, add sauce into the skillet and over the meatballs. Top with cheese and bake until meatballs are cooked through, about 10 minutes more.

  5. After meatballs have cooked through, garnish with parsley and serve!
